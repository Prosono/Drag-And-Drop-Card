## Design Context

### Users
Home Assistant dashboard builders and power users who arrange Lovelace cards on a freeform canvas. They use Drag & Drop Card to create polished wall panels, tablet dashboards, and room-specific control surfaces without losing direct control over layout, sizing, tabs, and live home data.

### Brand Personality
Premium, calm, precise. The interface should feel like a high-end smart home surface: confident, refined, and quietly capable rather than flashy or decorative.

### Aesthetic Direction
Premium smart home. Favor restrained glass, softened metal-like surfaces, clear hierarchy, crisp iconography, and subtle depth. The sidebar should feel integrated into the dashboard canvas, with calm status awareness and smooth interaction. Avoid toy-like neon, generic admin UI, heavy gradients, and visual clutter.

### Design Principles
- Make the sidebar feel like an embedded smart home control rail, not a separate settings widget.
- Prioritize glanceability: time, status, weather, people, and navigation should scan quickly.
- Use restrained depth and accent color; let Home Assistant theme tokens do most of the work.
- Keep controls touch-friendly for tablets and wall panels while preserving compact desktop density.
- Preserve accessibility through visible focus states, semantic buttons, and reduced-motion friendly transitions.
